# Elegant variation

LLMs avoid repeating a noun in consecutive sentences by reaching for a synonym. Humans usually don't — repetition is often clearer than the variation.

## The tell

> The dog ran across the field. The canine then **leaped** over the fence. The pooch was **enthused**.

Three different words for the same dog, two stretched verbs. The variation feels strenuous because it is.

## The fix

If repetition would be clearer than the variation, restore the repetition:

> The dog ran across the field. It jumped the fence. It was excited.

`The dog` → `it` works fine in English. So does repeating `the dog` directly. The synonym-reach is what flags as slop.

## When variation is fine

- Different things actually deserve different words (a "dog" and a "puppy" are different — vary).
- Technical contexts where the synonym carries a different shade of meaning.
- Long passages where repetition would genuinely become tedious — but use pronouns first, synonyms only if pronouns don't work.

## Heuristic

If you can replace the synonym with the original noun (or with a pronoun) and the sentence reads more naturally, you've found elegant variation. Restore the repetition.
