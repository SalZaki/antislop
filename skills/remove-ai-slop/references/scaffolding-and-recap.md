# Scaffolding and recap

The bare AI-isms that escape into pasted text, plus the recap-intros and recap-outros that frame ordinary writing as Important Content. Delete on sight.

## Leftover assistant scaffolding

Phrases that should have been stripped before paste:

- "Certainly!" / "Sure!" / "Absolutely!" at the start
- "I hope this helps!" / "Let me know if you have any other questions!" at the end
- "Here's a breakdown:" / "Here's a summary:" preceding the body
- "As an AI language model, I..." (rare but happens)
- "As of my last knowledge update / training data,"
- Mid-text apologies: "I apologize for the confusion."
- "Great question!" / "That's an excellent point!"
- "Let me know if you'd like me to elaborate on any of these points."

Fix: delete.

## Recap intros

Telltale openers that promise content instead of delivering it:

- "In this article, we'll explore..."
- "In this post, we'll dive into..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Let's take a look at..."
- "In today's [topic] landscape..."
- "In the world of [topic],"
- "Imagine a world where..."

Fix: delete the intro. Open with the first real sentence of the body.

## Recap outros

Telltale closers:

- "In conclusion,"
- "All in all,"
- "To sum up,"
- "Ultimately," (when meaning "in summary")
- "Despite challenges, X continues to..." (the canonical AI closer)
- "Time will tell, but..."
- "Only time will tell."
- "The future of X is bright."

Fix: delete the outro. If the body needs a final beat, write one direct sentence — don't recap.
